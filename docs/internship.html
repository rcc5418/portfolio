This page will record my experiences while doing my internship with the College Knowledge Foundtation as an Editor/Web Designer (Summer 2024)!

Week 1: 5/13 - 5/17
-Aquainted myself with various task-management/website design softwares to use with the team
-Attended my first meetings and met all my collegaues at College Knowledge.
-Completed various onboarding tasks, like reviewing the employee handbook and mission statement
This first week has been mostly about getting aqcuainted with the efficiency tools College Knowledge utilizes in their operations, like ClickUp and various Google services like Calendar. Something else I did was watch some TED Talk clips from Simon Senek, an inspirational speaker, about concepts like Infinite vs Finite players, and about focusing your motivation around the the 'Why'. Honestly, pretty interesting stuff!

Week 2: 5/20 - 5/24
-Met with the marketing team for the first time.
-Arranged future regular meetings with supervisor and teams.
-Began working on an updated website, using Wix's website builder.
This weeks biggest milestone has been beginning work at designing and creating an updated, modern website for the College Knowledge foundation. So far, I've just been creating a prototype homepage using some design principles inspired by some speakers' websites. The website is currently hosted here, but I haven't published any changes yet, and probably won't for a while.

Week 3: 5/27 - 5/31
-Polished up the website's homepage redesign.
-Edited my first short for the Foundation, including re-edits/adjustments .
This weeks biggest milestone was definitely beginning my editing work creating short-form video content for the College Knowledge Foundation. I'm doing this by using CapCut, which is an editing software I hadn't worked with before, but it's actually pretty simple and intuitive, so I think I've gotten the hang of it quickly. I'm looking forward to making more videos!

Week 4: 6/3 - 6/7
-Tweaked the website's menu and moved forward with more page redesigns.
-Edited three more reels, expirementing with different variables and animations
This week, we've planned alot for more operations in the future, including me possibly going to an in-person College Knowledge information session to record video, which is pretty exciting. Other than that, I worked a lot more with CapCut to polish the short videos, and look forward to adjusting our formula more over the coming weeks.

Week 5: 6/10 - 6/14
-Redesigned more web pages.
-Learned more about Wix's capabilities, adjusting list items to create a smoother user experience when exploring the site.
-Created a prototype loop animation for use in our reels in Adobe Premiere.
We've been planning ahead a bit when it comes to future summer operations, including the prospect of me acting as a videographer for a future College Knowledge operation in July. Other than that, we've also been discussing different options for specific website optics and marketing content. I'm looking forward to creating more kinds of content for the socials!

Week 6: 6/17 - 6/21
-Started the website re-haul, creating many new pages and restructuring existing ones
-Began working with the podcast content, creating long-form videos and planning additional reel content
This week has been a bit difficult as I've been assigned more work than previous weeks while other obstacles have appeared in my life, but learning to juggle these tasks is part of the growing I'm hoping to achieve over this summer. Regardless, there's lots of work to be done in the next week!

Week 7: 6/24 - 6/28
-Followed through with website rehaul, creating more new pages and ensuring they link together competently
This week has been all about making sure the website is ready for an influx of users after Donovin starts doing some in-person sessions at Lycoming College next week. This has involved some close discussion with Donovin Lindsay on how to display some of our services, as well as the assurance the rehaul would be complete by next week. The website is in a good place right now. There are several pages I wish to redesign still, but nothing of urgency. Next week I should be able to work more on short/podcast video content while preparing to record one of Donovin's sessions in person in two weeks.

Week 8: 7/1 - 7/5
-Continuted development of video content, including integrating correct branding when necessary and evolving the short video process.
Much of the important website content has been completed, with more pages being planned on being rewritten/redesigned but waiting on content to move forward with it. In lieu of other pertinent tasks, I've been working on developing College Knowledge's video content for their social media platforms, including podcast content. Next week, I hope to do more work like this while also meeting with Donovin and recoring one of his presentations.

Week 9: 7/8 - 7/12 BIRTHDAY WEEK!
-Continued developing video content for various social media platforms, with various types of videos produced.
-Designed/developed further web pages using content written by other team members
-Met with supervisor to record live lecture content at the Pennsylvania Free Enterprise Week.
This week I have been a man of many hats, but I appreciate the variety! The way I see it, by performing these different tasks it keeps each individual skillset sharp. Next week I should do more of the same, possibly editing the content I recorded at PFEW for future use.

Week 10: 7/15 - 7/19
-Continued development of video content for social media
-Began rehaul of several website pages for mobile view
-Handled lecture video content to ensure high quality footage for later use
This week I've been handling a lot of what I'd call busy work, which is mostly just following up on other tasks I'd begun earlier, like editing short videos and handling website content differently. I'm not sure what will come up next, but the next week may bring further web-page redesigns/rehauls along with more videos to be edited.

Week 10: 7/22 - 7/26
-Concluded website rehaul for mobile viewing, ensuring consistent quality over multiple platforms
-Edited new podcast content, including longform and shortform content to be used for promotion
There's more website work to be done, but much of it will be done in collaboration with other people on the marketing team. Additionally, we're beginning a new season of the podcast, so I have some more content to handle in the meantime, which is what Ill be working on this week. I've also discussed my position with my supervisor and decided to stay on until the Summer's conclusion, possibly staying on in a smaller position during the school year.
