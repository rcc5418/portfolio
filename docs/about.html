About Me!

Reece Cullen (that's me) is a Digital Media, Arts, and Technology (DIGIT) student at Penn State Behrend, the Erie College. Currently a senior, he plans to go into game design and development.
On this page, I'll put whatever show I'm watching, whatever book I'm reading, or whatever game I'm playing. I thought it would be fun to give you a eensy glimpse into my life beyond my work!



Currently Playing: Darkest Dungeon II
I was so excited when I found out that Darkest Dungeon had a sequel when I started getting into it, but I'm a bit dissapointed. The artstyle flourishes far more than I thought it would in three dimensions, and overall I like the gameplay's transition from chaotic and random to intensly strategic. But there are some gripes that really get in my way of enjoying the game! For one, there are so many status effects and menus, and I found them all far more difficult to parse than the previous game. Additionally, this game feels way slower than DD1. It feels like offense has been nerfed and defensive strategies have been buffed, so oftentimes, buckling down and taking the long game is the most viable strategy. HOWEVER, numerous enemies pierce your defenses with DOTs, like bleed blight or burn, and the further into the game you progress, the more the enemies are buffed with massive healthpools which ENSURE that EVERY SINGLE BATTLE lasts TEN MINUTES OR MORE
Anyway I can't recommend the game unless you like unfair experiences and overcoming the odds. I'll still beat it though.



Currently Watching: Your Honor
Your Honor is a show that feels carried by the cast, but that's always felt like a fake criticism, you know? It's like calling a movie emotionally manipulative because of the music, or something. As a drama, Your Honor is both crazy and predictable, exciting and boring, a true contradiction of a series. The writing is lacking, which I'd cite as the main reason I'm not more passionate about the show. The twists and turns feel too choreagraphed, and some of the scenario-writing is fundamentally flawed. If you really like Bryan Cranston, I'd check it out!
