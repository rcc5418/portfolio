About Me!

Reece Cullen (that's me) is a Digital Media, Arts, and Technology (DIGIT) student at Penn State Behrend, the Erie College. Currently a junior,
he plans to go into game design and development.



On this page, I'll put whatever show I'm watching, whatever book I'm reading, or whatever game I'm playing.

I thought it would be fun to give you a eensy glimpse into my life beyond my work!



Currently Playing: Professor Layton vs Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney

I've been a fan of Ace Attorney for a long time by now, and I've played (and loved) all the Professor Layton games that were on
the Nintendo DS, but I'd never actually played this crossover title. It took a Let's Play channel's playthrough of the series
to finally get me to check it out myself. I knew I would enjoy their videos, but moreso if I had played the game previously. It's pretty good so far,
I'm a huge fan of puzzle and mystery games where you have to figure out the solution yourself.



Currently Watching: The Last of Us on HBO Max

The Last of Us is honestly one of the best games I've ever played, and its sequel is pretty good too! When I heard that they'd
be making a TV show adaptation, I was excited, but it's hard to get hyped for something that seems so far away. Well as of now, the show is finally coming out with a new
episode every week, and it's been incredible so far. If you're a fan of dystopian fiction, zombies, found family plots, or just plain well-written drama, I'd recommend it!



Currently Reading: The Ultimate Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy

When I was younger, I would read all the time, but I don't carve out time to read as much these days. I still buy tons
of books, however. A recent purchase was The Ultimate Hitchiker's Guide to the Galaxy, a book I'd wanted to read for a while. It contains all five books in the series
(which I didn't know existed, really), and it's been okay so far. Something has been nagging me about it, though. In the foreword, there's notes about how the author, Douglas Adams,
never really wanted to be an author, and resented his series' success. I might be imagining things when I read it, but I feel as if I can detect a hint of that cynicism througout the books.
